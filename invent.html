Inventions

  • A drone for searching for people

    In 2020, Albatros introduced a new autonomous aerial search and rescue system (or simply a drone) that combines proven systems with new artificial intelligence technologies.

    The neural network, which is built into the camera and microcomputer of the drone, recognizes the features and identification marks of the missing person and automatically transmits the data to the search teams.

    This development helps to search for missing people both high in the mountains and in the deepest caves, and will help to significantly reduce the search area in a short time.

  • The first domestic MRI machine

    The first magnetic resonance imaging device was created at the Lebedev Physical Institute of the Russian Academy of Sciences in 2022, and it has become a real challenge for scientists.

    Although you can't take an MRI scan into space, its development was as challenging as producing space satellites. There were many different systems that needed to be developed, and each one required the involvement of a wide range of specialists, from doctors to mathematicians and programmers.

    The main part of the device, the magnet, is made of Russian-made superconductors, which has reduced the weight of the device by several tons compared to previous-generation devices.

    In addition, the Russian version is cheaper than its counterparts and is capable of taking better pictures. Scientists are ready to start mass production of the development within three years.

  • The use of robobacks in construction

    Previously, robot dogs were only used for entertainment, but now they help people explore dangerous areas and mine minerals.

    In 2023, 3Logic Group made a real breakthrough in the construction industry: the robot dogs scanned the space and helped to obtain 3D models of objects. The robots were able to conduct a full screening of a construction site in Tyumen in just 12 minutes. In comparison: it would take a surveyor up to 72 hours to complete this task.

    This application of robots in construction improves data accuracy, reduces costs, and improves the quality of work. In the future, this will become common practice and can increase the competitiveness of the industry and help to free surveyors from routine work in favor of more comprehensive research.
